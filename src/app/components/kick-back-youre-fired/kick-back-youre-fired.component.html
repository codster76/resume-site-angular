Kick Back: You're Fired
Sorry, mobile users, but this game is only functional on desktops. (it kind of works on mobile, but wasn't intended for it)

Controls:

Shoot: Left-Click
Switch Weapons: Right-Click

What is it?

My team's submission for the GMTK Game Jam 2023, where the theme was "roles reversed". This was made in 48 hours using Unity.
We thought hard about how to substantially incorporate the theme, which led to a bunch of ideas - with my favourite scrapped idea being a retro FPS where you'd throw newspapers back at delivery boys as payback for spamming you with junk mail. The one we chose in the end was a lot better, though, since the use of the theme was threefold and was both mechanically and thematically consistent:

- You fire a gun to move and you move to deal damage. Your bullets are harmless, but ramming into people with the recoil is deadly.
- You are literally riding an office chair in reverse.
- You are a disgraced CEO "climbing" back up the corporate chain.

Design Thoughts

The game was pretty heavily inspired by Hotline Miami with the intended approach to the levels, just with a bit more of a focus on traversal, rather than combat.

The game was designed to be hard to control, yet satisfying, which I think was achieved reasonably well. It was a fine balance between weapon force, fire rate, character drag and character bounce.

Enemy design was pretty challenging because we couldn't make them too smart of powerful, yet we still needed them to be an obstacle. I put a lot of time into making the ranged enemies behave in a properly. They needed to attack you on sight and search for you when you're out of sight and I wanted to make it look convincing. My friend Hugh made the hunter enemy (the really fast red guy near the end of the game), which ended up working out great, with it crashing into walls and flying past corners due to speed and inertia. It really achieved a natural-feeling chase sequence.
