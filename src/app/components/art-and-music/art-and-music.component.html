Mega Man 7 8-Bit Soundtrack Project

This was probably the biggest personal project I've completed. It's a comprehensive cover of the entire Mega Man 7 soundtrack in 8-bit (NES 2A03 + VRC6). I started some time in 2018 and finished it in May 2023. Obviously, I wasn't working on it for the entire time, but I'd chip away at it slowly over the years, going back and endlessly adjusting things as I improved. It was a passion project in the truest sense. All of the music was manually transcribed and recreated, all of the art was drawn by me (it is substantially based on in-game assets and official art, so I can't take all the credit), all the video editing was done by me, and the gameplay at the end is the very same Mega Man game that you can demo on this site.

I legitimately went into excruciating detail to get everything perfect. The non-gameplay segments of the video should be pixel-perfect in every single frame, meaning that no pixel ever deviates from the defined grid. Even the scrolling text is perfect. On that note, the scrolling text was generated programmatically. I wrote a small C# program that takes text inputs and an image and spits out a sequence of images of the text scrolling across the input image. I might release it one day, but it's in a pretty crude state right now. Feel free to email me about it if you really want to see it, though. I'll be happy to show you.

Other Music

My primary musical skill is transcription, which is what I use to make covers of songs. I use this to make 8-bit versions of songs, using a tool called FamiTracker. It simulates the limitations of the Nintendo Entertainment System’s audio, allowing for authentic 8-bit music. It’s a bit cumbersome to use, but some great chiptunes have come out of it (the soundtrack of the game Shovel Knight was made using this if you want to see an example of professional usage of it). I also transcribe music into a tool called Musescore, used for writing musical scores for various instruments. I primarily use piano (though, I can’t actually play the instrument).
